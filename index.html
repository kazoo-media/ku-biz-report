📋 Performance Summary

Meta · Mar 26 – May 18 LinkedIn · Mar 26 – May 18

The KU School of Business 2026 paid media campaign has delivered strong results across Meta and LinkedIn since launch in late March. Through May 18, the campaign has generated over 3.6 million total impressions and 12,900+ link clicks across both platforms, targeting business executives and alumni across Kansas City, AZ/Phoenix, Wichita, Chicago, and Dallas.

Meta is consistently performing above the education industry benchmark of 1.3–2.0% CTR. In April, the KC audience drove a 1.97% CTR — nearly hitting the top of the benchmark range. May has seen the campaign expand to four concurrent markets simultaneously, with all audiences tracking solidly within or above benchmark.

LinkedIn performance is consistent with KU Brand ad benchmarks across all audiences and months. Dwell times average 17+ seconds in April and May, indicating strong content engagement from the professional audience. The LinkedIn impression scale expanded significantly in April (1.9M impressions) as targeting was refined and budgets were optimized.

Recommendations

Future Media Strategy

Based on campaign performance through May 18, 2026, the following recommendations inform how we should approach future media planning and investment for the KU School of Business.

Refresh KC Creative in the Next Flight

Kansas City has been the campaign's strongest-performing market, but CTR has declined each month (2.14% → 1.97% → 1.47%), which is a typical sign of audience fatigue. For future flights, we recommend introducing new creative variations in the KC market to re-engage this audience and sustain the strong performance we've seen from them throughout the campaign.

Expand Chicago & Dallas in Future Buys

Both Chicago and Dallas launched in May and immediately hit benchmark CTR — Chicago at 1.51% and Dallas at 1.55% — in their very first month. This is a strong early signal that these markets are receptive to KU's messaging. Future media plans should prioritize increasing investment in both markets to capitalize on this early traction.

Position LinkedIn as a Brand Awareness Channel

LinkedIn is delivering strong brand awareness value for KU, with audiences averaging 17+ seconds of dwell time — a meaningful engagement signal from a highly targeted professional audience. Going forward, LinkedIn should be planned and evaluated as an awareness channel, with dwell time and impression reach as the primary success metrics rather than click volume.

Use This Campaign as a Baseline for 2027 Planning

This campaign has established clear performance benchmarks across five markets and two platforms. With LinkedIn already exceeding its impression goal (134%) and the overall campaign tracking to surpass 3.51M total impressions, the 2026 campaign provides a strong data foundation for setting goals, allocating budget by market, and prioritizing platforms for next year's media plan.
